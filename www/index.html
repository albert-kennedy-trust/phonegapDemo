Albert Kennedy Trust

Supporting Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Trans young people 25 years old and younger who are homeless or living in a hostile environment.

How we can help

Our priority is your safety. When we can’t help you to stay safely at home, we may be able to offer you help with a safe and supportive place to stay.

Safe Homes

Our Purple Door project has a mix of emergency, medium and long term accommodation in London, Manchester and Newcastle.

This includes our Independent Living project in Newcastle and Northumberland which offers a mix of self-contained flats and bedsits for up to 18 months.

Our Supported Lodgings project has a network of volunteer carers in London and Manchester who can provide a room in a safe home and family environment.

AKT also works with housing providers in London, Manchester & Newcastle who may also be able to meet your need for safe housing. Throughout your stay with AKT we will help you get your life back on track and move on.

Emergency Support

When forced into a period of homelessness there are often more issues than just finding somewhere to live. AKT could help you with practical and financial support to stay off the streets and to maintain positive networks and routines.

Rainbow Starter Pack

Living on your own is tough! We may be able to support you with moving onto independent living by helping reduce the cost of living in your own place.

Other services

Our support doesn’t end when you find somewhere to live. We will continue to work with you both at our offices and elsewhere to help you make the right choices for you and to ensure that homelessness is a thing of the past - not the future.

Mentoring

AKT has a team of trained mentors who can help you move your life forward positively

Mentoring can help you with:

  • Coming to terms with your identity
  • Helping your parents understand
  • Job hunting
  • Juggling your money
  • Keeping on top of school or college work
  • Meeting other people your age who understand you
And lots more. The help they give will depend on what you want and need.

On your side

If you feel frustrated or unable to talk to people in authority when asking for help, AKT may be able to help you get the support and services you are entitled to.

Our team of staff and volunteer advocates know how to get the best for you. If you are unhappy with how you are being treated they will write letters of support, come with you to meetings, talk to decision makers and can explain your rights to make sure they aren’t ignored.

Training

We run a whole range of life skills and tenancy training through which AKT can offer you:
  • Job Skills
  • CV Writing
  • Confidence Building
  • Cooking and Household Maintenance
  • Telephone Skills
  • Making the Most of Your Budget
  • Managing Difficult Situations
During these sessions you will learn new skills, gain qualifications and meet other young people who understand what you are going through.

About us

Since 1989 The Albert Kennedy Trust (AKT) has supported Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Trans (LGBT) young people 25 years old and younger who are homeless or living in a hostile environment.

If you are 25 or younger, and identify as Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Trans, Queer, Questioning or Intersex, and you are:

  • Homeless or in crisis at home
  • Facing bullying and / or rejection
  • In need of support around coming out
Or if:
  • You are supporting a young person who might need our help
We may be able to help you.
